About Me

I am a senior at the University of Southern California studying mathematics (with a minor in physics). My research interests include algebraic geometry (for example, areas like birational geometry, homotopical algebraic geometry, and arithmetic geometry), algebraic topology (broadly, homotopy theory, homological mirror symmetry, and knot theory), and mathematical/theoretical physics (the geometrical aspects of quantum gravity including mirror symmetry and holography in string theory). These interests are ever-evolving, but they are things I enjoy studying and talking about at the moment. Starting this Fall, I will be an NSF Graduate Research Fellow at the University of Michigan!

I maintain this page to keep track of (and easily communicate) my writing and reading. I might eventually add a blog section to the page... that said, a lot of this webpage is still under construction. Write to me if anything you see intrigues you!
